FAQ

Common questions

Straight answers for teams evaluating an enterprise communications archive.

What is a communications archive?

A communications archive is a system that captures, stores, and preserves every message an organization sends to its customers, so the organization can retrieve and prove exactly what was sent years later. Unlike a backup, an archive is immutable: records are write-once and tamper-evident, which is what regulators and courts require. Enterprises use communications archives to meet records-retention rules, respond to legal discovery, and resolve customer disputes with the precise message a recipient received.

Can Fahos archive messages sent through Adobe Journey Optimizer or Salesforce Marketing Cloud?

Yes. Fahos is built for enterprises running platforms such as Adobe Journey Optimizer, Salesforce Marketing Cloud, Adobe Campaign, Braze, Marketo Engage and Iterable. It connects to your platform's delivery feed and archives every message it sends, so your marketing and transactional communications are preserved in one immutable, searchable system regardless of which platform or channel produced them.

Does it cover transactional and marketing messages across all channels?

Yes. Fahos archives both marketing communications (campaigns, newsletters, promotions) and transactional communications (statements, receipts, verification codes, service notices) across every channel your platforms send through — email, SMS, push, in-app, WhatsApp and direct mail. Each message is preserved exactly as the recipient received it, with its channel and metadata recorded for search and retrieval.

How fast can I retrieve an archived message?

Any single record opens in well under a second — typically around 400 milliseconds — even when it has been archived for years in the coldest storage tier. Fahos indexes the exact byte location of every message, so retrieval reads only that one record rather than restoring or scanning a large archive. There is no restore queue and no thaw delay, which is common with conventional cold-storage archives.

Which regulations does an enterprise communications archive help with?

A communications archive supports retention and production obligations including SEC Rule 17a-4(f) and FINRA recordkeeping in financial services, MiFID II in the EU, and broad data-protection and records laws such as GDPR. Fahos provides write-once (WORM) immutability, SHA-256 chain-of-custody verification, legal holds, and defensible export with sealed manifests — the controls auditors and regulators look for. Always confirm specific regulatory fit with your compliance team.

How is this different from traditional per-mailbox email archiving?

Traditional archives were designed for human inboxes and priced per mailbox or per seat. That model breaks for enterprises that send billions of machine-generated, personalized messages from a handful of platforms — there are no mailboxes to count. Fahos is priced and engineered for that volume: it archives platform output directly, tiers records to low-cost storage while keeping retrieval instant, and covers every channel rather than email alone.
